Seed Systems for Pasture and Forage Development in Nigeria: A Goldagric Initiative

The Goldagric-led project on enhancing seed systems for pasture and forage development in Nigeria has made significant strides in addressing the challenges faced by the livestock sector. By increasing the availability of quality forage seeds, building local capacity, and creating awareness, the project has laid a strong foundation for sustainable livestock production and improved livelihoods for smallholder farmers.

  • Impact and Outcomes

    Enhanced local capacity: The capacity of local institutions and farmers in forage seed production and management was strengthened, ensuring the sustainability of the seed system.
    Increased adoption of improved forages: The project created awareness among farmers about the benefits of improved forages, leading to increased adoption and better livestock management practices.

  • Project Productivity Process

    Goldagric has a dedicated processing facility equipped with necessary equipment such as seed cleaning machines, gravity separators, and graders. For this ICRC project, Goldagric cleaned the harvested seeds to remove impurities, chaff, and other plant debris. Goldagric conducted quality control tests to ensure purity, germination rate, and moisture content meet established standards. During the Packaging process Goldagric selected appropriate packaging materials such as high-quality seed bags, labels, and seals. Goldagric package the processed seeds in bags, clearly indicating the variety, lot number, and other essential information. Goldagric implemented quality control measures during the packaging process to maintain uniformity and prevent contamination. By prioritizing good seed production processes done by the help of Goldagric, ICRC can support the establishment of sustainable agricultural systems in affected communities.

Good seed production processes play a critical role in addressing humanitarian needs and the International Committee of the Red Cross strives to meet its mandate in that area. Partnering with Goldagric they provide the foundation for food security, resilience, disease resistance, adaptation, livelihood improvement, and long-term sustainability in communities affected by humanitarian crises like war and hunger. By investing in Goldagrics quality seeds, ICRC organization can contribute to the overall well-being and recovery of these communities, ensuring a pathway to self-sufficiency and long-term resilience.
